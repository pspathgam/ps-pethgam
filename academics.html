Curriculum Structure (Pre-Primary to Class 5)

Our academic program follows the curriculum prescribed by the State Board of School Education, with a strong focus on the goals of **Foundational Literacy and Numeracy (FLN)**. Learning is primarily activity-based and uses local context for maximum relevance.

  • **Pre-Primary/Aanganwadi (Ages 3-5):** Focus on cognitive development, language stimulation, gross motor skills, and preparatory numeracy.
  • **Grades 1 & 2:** Intensive focus on foundational reading, writing (local language and English), and basic counting/arithmetic skills.
  • **Grades 3 to 5:** Introduction to more formal subjects, including Environmental Studies (EVS), Social Studies basics, and structured moral/value education.
Grade Level Core Subjects Learning Approach
Pre-Primary Aanganwadi syllabus (Play-way, Storytelling) Activity-based, Sensory Learning
Grades 1 - 2 Language (Primary/English), Mathematics FLN focus, Hands-on Material (TLM)
Grades 3 - 5 Language, Math, Environmental Science (EVS), Social Studies, Art Structured Learning, Group Projects, Quizzes

Examination and Assessment Policy

Assessment is continuous and developmental. We follow the prescribed Uniform Academic Calendar for examinations, ensuring fair and comprehensive evaluation of student progress, especially focusing on FLN skills.

Assessment Type Timeline Focus Area
**Baseline Assessment** Beginning of Session (April/May) Initial FLN competency level
Unit Tests / Formative Monthly/Bimonthly In-class progress, activity assessment
**Mid-Term Examination** September/October Comprehensive Coverage (50%)
**Annual Examination** March Final Comprehensive Evaluation (100%)

**Important Note:** Results for individual students are available via the 'Our Heroes' (Students) section or the Admin Panel.
